When did I start
liking anime?
As an artist who often incorporates anime-style elements into my artwork, many people ask me how I developed a passion for this unique art form. The truth is, my love for anime goes back to my childhood days, when I first discovered the world of Japanese animation. I vividly remember stumbling upon an anime series on TV one Saturday morning. The vibrant colors, the expressive characters, and the captivating storylines instantly grabbed my attention. From that moment on, I was hooked.
As a child, I eagerly devoured every anime series I could find. I spent hours glued to the screen, immersed in the fantastical worlds created by talented animators. I was captivated by the intricate details of the characters' designs, the emotive expressions, and the dynamic action sequences.
As I grew older, my love for anime deepened. I began to appreciate the artistry and creativity that went into each frame of animation. I marveled at the intricate backgrounds, the fluid movements, and the attention to detail in every aspect of the storytelling. Anime also ignited my own creativity as an artist. I was inspired by the unique character designs, the use of color and composition, and the storytelling techniques employed in anime. I started incorporating anime-inspired elements into my own artwork, experimenting with different styles and techniques to create my own artistic voice.
